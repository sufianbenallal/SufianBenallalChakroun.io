Predictive Location System for Network Outlets — Professional Football Stadium

Developed during internship at BOINSCA, S.L.
  • Created an analytics prototype to estimate the location of network outlets without coordinates in a professional football stadium infrastructure.
  • Consolidated and cleaned more than 10,000 CommScope imVision, cabling, rack and ecometry records into a structured dataset for model training and validation.
  • Compared KNN, Random Forest, Extra Trees, Gradient Boosting and Medoid approaches, then combined Medoid with an Extra Trees + HistGradientBoosting ensemble through an agreement-based selector.
  • Validated the system on 2,027 cases, achieving a median error of 9.43 plan units and reducing extreme errors from 27 to 7 compared with the pure Medoid baseline.
  • Prioritised 133 high-confidence outlets for technician validation and generated floor-level AutoCAD/DXF overlays for field review.
Pythonscikit-learnpandasEnsemble LearningOutlier Detection

Solar Orientation Estimation from Shadow Analysis

Developed with the GRAM Research Group, University of Alcalá
  • Developed a computer vision pipeline to estimate solar orientation from real shadow images, combining geometric annotation, dataset preparation and deep learning-based angular regression.
  • Built the MATLAB-assisted annotation workflow using vanishing points, reference directions and real shadow angles to generate consistent ground-truth labels.
  • Fine-tuned pre-trained Vision Transformer (ViT) models and compared angular representations and training strategies for robust orientation estimation.
  • Produced auxiliary scripts for image capture, dataset organisation, metric evaluation and experiment control, achieving an approximate final error of 5°.
Computer VisionMATLABPyTorchVision TransformerAngular Regression

LoRa-Based Indoor Positioning System — University Building

Developed with the GRAM Research Group, University of Alcalá
  • Designed and tested an indoor positioning prototype using four distributed LoRa transmitters and RSSI measurements collected at known reference points inside a university building.
  • Mapped transmitter and receiver coordinates onto the university floor plan, converting pixel locations into metric 3D coordinates and accounting for floor-height differences.
  • Processed LoRa message logs in MATLAB, extracting RSSI values from received packets, filtering measurements with moving median techniques and computing average RSSI per transmitter.
  • Modelled the RSSI-distance relationship through calibration references and estimated path-loss parameters to infer distances from new signal-strength measurements.
  • Implemented a 3D multilateration routine based on four-sphere intersection to estimate the receiver position from distances to the transmitters.
LoRaRSSIMATLABIndoor Positioning3D Multilateration
